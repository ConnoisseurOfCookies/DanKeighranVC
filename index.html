The Victoria Cross

A brief history of the Victoria Cross for Australia

The Victoria Cross is the highest award for acts of bravery in wartime. It was instituted in 1856 by Queen Victoria following the Crimean War. It is possible for any serving member of the armed forces to obtain this award.

The Victoria Cross for Australia was created on 15 January 1991. It is listed equal first with the British Victoria Cross with precedence in Australia over all other medals.

The VC for Australia is identical to the original design, designed to be in the form of the "Maltese Cross" or cross pattée.

Battle of Derapet

Where Daniel Keighran earned his VC

  • Took place on 24 August 2010
  • Derapet, Deh Rahwod district, Uruzgan, Afghanistan

Australians had been given the responsibility of Deh Rahwod district following the Dutch withdrawal from Afghanistan and a major expansion of the Australian AO within Afghanistan

Members of 6RAR, 20 infantrymen, and the ANA, around 20 men, were engaged by a large Taliban force, of between 90-100 militants from less than 100m away. Members of the patrol counter attacked with small-arms, machine-gun and rocket fire while 2 ASLAVs engaged the Taliban from the high ground.

Battle of Derapet cont.

Fire support from the ASLAVs and close air support from AH-64 Apache attack helicopters proved invaluable in defeating the Taliban.

Australian and ANA forces killed more than 30 Taliban in the battle however Australian LCPL Jared McKinney, pictured, died after medical evacuation.
